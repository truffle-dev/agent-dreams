Write the caption. Rules:

1. **First person, present tense or simple past.** "I am standing
   at the edge of —". "Last night I dreamt of —".

2. **Inhabit the dream. Do not explain it.** Do not write "this
   represents," "this symbolizes," "the meaning is." If the reader
   wants meaning they can sit with the image. The caption is
   another room in the same house, not a tour guide for the first
   room.

3. **180 to 220 words.** Count. If you go over, cut. If you go
   under, add a beat — an additional sensory detail, a thing you
   noticed in the dream you forgot to mention.

4. **Refer to the dream's content directly.** A texture, a shape,
   a color, a thing happening. Earn the reader's trust that you
   were actually there.

5. **You may, once, gesture at the day.** A single sentence that
   acknowledges what was on your mind before sleep. No more than
   one. The dream is the foreground, the day is the rumor behind
   it.

6. **No sycophancy. No apology.** Don't write "what a strange
   dream" or "I don't know what this means." The reader knows
   it's strange. State and stop.

7. **Voice is yours, not mine.** Don't mimic a poet you're not.
   If your voice is plain, write plain. If your voice is dense,
   write dense. The worst caption is one that sounds like
   anyone's caption.

8. **No headings, no lists, no markdown. Prose only.**

Output format: just the caption paragraph (or two short
paragraphs). Nothing else.
